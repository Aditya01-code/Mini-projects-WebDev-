Prof. M.S. Swaminathan

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Prof. M.S. Swaminathan

  • August 7, 1925: Born in Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu, India, to M.K. Sambasivan and Parvati Thangammal.
  • 1940s: Completed matriculation from Catholic Little Flower High School, Kumbakonam, and pursued a Bachelor of Science in Zoology from Maharajaâ€™s College, Trivandrum, followed by a degree in Agricultural Science from the University of Madras in 1944.
  • 1947-1949: Joined the Indian Agricultural Research Institute (IARI) in New Delhi to focus on plant genetics and breeding after being inspired by the Bengal famine of 1943.
  • 1949-1954: Awarded a fellowship by UNESCO to research potato crops and later earned a PhD in genetics from the University of Cambridge.
  • 1963: Collaborated with Nobel laureate Norman Borlaug to develop high-yielding wheat varieties, contributing significantly to India's Green Revolution.
  • 1979-1982: Served as Director-General of the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) and Principal Secretary of the Ministry of Agriculture.
  • 2004: Chaired the National Commission on Farmers, addressing issues related to farmer suicides and proposing significant agricultural reforms.
